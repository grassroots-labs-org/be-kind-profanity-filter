Seeds crack open in charred soil. Life persists, and you can too.

Trauma recovery is not about rebuilding the old you. It’s about rewilding, returning to the untamed essence of who you are, guided by Earth’s quiet wisdom, you can do this through connecting with Nature. This journey is not a race but a slow dance, moving to the rhythm of your body, breath, your heartbeat, and the pulse of the living world. You will find balance through the Earth’s Natural Pulse. The Earth remembers you, even when you’ve lost sight of yourself. She holds the blueprint of your wholeness, etched in the electric currents of your being, its all there still intact, an invisible vibrating energy field. Trauma is a fracture, but it is also a summons, a call to shed the skin of who you thought you were and step into who you might become. In a world that prizes speed and surface, this process feels radical. It demands you slow down, listen, and honour the sacred tempo of your grief. Like a river carving stone, healing reshapes you over time, revealing strength in your softness and wisdom in your wounds.

Your body knows this truth. Beneath the ache and pain, an invisible energy field hums—a bioelectric map of your wholeness, discovered by scientists like Harold Burr in the 1940s. When you feel broken, this field vibrates with light, the message is: You are still whole. You are still here.

To understand and feel this, you must turn to the Nature. She is not just a backdrop but a co-healer, a living presence that mirrors your resilience. Her trees breathe with you. Her soil grounds your scattered thoughts. Her rivers carry your tears. In her embrace, you begin to clean away any toxic energy and rewild your soul, reclaiming the wild purity of nature’s essence is remembering how healing is done, its that simple, it’s just that intuitive part of you that modern life has tamed through making you comfortable in your separation from nature.

Tools for Rewilding the Soul

Rewilding after trauma is a personal pilgrimage, weaving together practical tools and sacred practices. Each step is an offering—to yourself, to the Earth, to the life that still flows through you.

Grounding the body: Trauma often traps you in loops of memory or fear on a cellular level, disconnecting you from the present. Somatic practices like qigong breathing or gentle yoga realign your nervous system, much like roots anchoring a tree, moving the body and even hypnotherapy is very useful for giving the body permission to release trauma on a deep subconscious level, ( I have a hypnotherapy trauma release healing program for this utilising hypnotherapy for subconscious cellular release, message me if you want a free consultation to learn more).









Grounding with Breathing Exercises and Drumming Circles

Stand barefoot on grass, inhale for four counts, exhale for six, breathing in through the belly and out from there also, this is the basis for qigong breathing, the stomach area being the place of the life force energy and chakras which often close up during trauma. Feel the Earth’s pulse beneath your feet, syncing your breath to hers.

Drumming is also a profoundly powerful tool for trauma and somatic healing because it bypasses the thinking mind and speaks directly to the body’s and Earth’s innate rhythms, its one of the best ways to align with the Earth’s pulse. The steady beat of a drum can help regulate the nervous system, grounding the body in the present moment and releasing stored tension from trauma.

Studies have shown that rhythmic drumming activates both hemispheres of the brain, facilitating emotional release, nonverbal expression, and deep integration. In Ecuador, I co-hosted a biweekly drumming circle for people and many where healing from trauma. We gathered as strangers with different stories, speaking few words, yet with the language of rhythm we shared deeply through our drumming. Without judgment, we wove our individual beats into a collective heartbeat, creating a potent somatic medicine. After each session, we returned home feeling more grounded, realigned, and whole—proof that when words fail, our bodies can still speak the language of healing. As a form of embodied medicine, drumming reconnects us to our primal heartbeat as well as the Earth’s heartbeat, reminding us we are alive, resilient, powerful and rooted, capable of healing.




Seek Nature’s Sanctuary

The Earth is a healer who asks nothing in return. Spend time in peaceful wild places—a forest, a beach, a quiet park, a garden if you can. Digging in soil, planting seeds, or tending herbs is a tactile prayer, grounding you in cycles of growth and renewal.
Scientific studies show that gardening reduces cortisol and fosters a sense of purpose.

Inspired by Indigenous traditions, create rituals to mark your healing. Gather stones, feathers, or leaves to build a small altar. Offer gratitude to the Earth, perhaps through an Andean despacho ceremony, where you bundle prayers in paper and return them to the land, I will be teaching these in the future and their significance in opening an honouring dialogue with nature. These acts restore connection and remind you: You are never alone, there are nature guardians taking care of you.

Trauma silences your voice, but creativity rekindles it. Paint, dance, write, sing, build—anything that channels your pain into presence. Like a bird weaving a nest, you are crafting a new home for your spirit.
